# Integrated Management Solutions for Durga Traders' Challenges

A Business Data Management (BDM) Capstone Project focused on analyzing operational and financial challenges faced by **Durga Traders**, a B2B coal wholesaler, using data analytics and business intelligence techniques.

---

## Project Overview

Durga Traders is a coal wholesaler based in Aligarh, Uttar Pradesh, supplying products such as:

- Lam Coke
- Met Coke
- Charcoal
- Pig Iron

The business primarily operates on credit-based transactions and sources coal from multiple regions including West Bengal, Haldia, and Odisha.

This project analyzes real business data to identify operational inefficiencies and provide practical, data-driven recommendations for improving profitability, liquidity, and inventory management.

---

## Problem Statement

The project addresses three major business challenges:

- Fluctuating purchase prices causing unstable profit margins
- Heavy dependence on credit-based sales leading to cash flow issues
- Coal quality deterioration during the monsoon season affecting profitability

---

## Objectives

- Analyze product-wise profit margins
- Study purchase and sales trends
- Evaluate cash flow and liquidity position
- Examine debtor and creditor management
- Assess the impact of seasonal quality variation
- Recommend practical business solutions backed by data

---

## Dataset

The analysis uses real business records provided by Durga Traders for the period:

**April 2022 – March 2023**

Data sources include:

- Purchase Ledger
- Sales Ledger
- Debtors Sheet
- Creditors Sheet
- Balance Sheet
- Stock Records

---

## Data Preparation

The dataset was cleaned and standardized by:

- Removing inconsistencies
- Standardizing product names
- Converting text fields into numerical values
- Creating calculated fields such as:
  - Profit Margin (%)
  - Monthly Sales
  - Monthly Purchases
  - Working Capital
  - Liquidity Ratios

## Key Findings

- Met Coke generated the highest average profit margins.
- Charcoal and Pig Iron produced the lowest returns.
- Profit margins declined during the monsoon due to moisture affecting coal quality.
- The company maintained healthy liquidity but relied heavily on supplier credit.
- Delayed customer payments affected cash flow despite positive profitability.
- Better supplier management and improved credit policies can significantly improve financial performance.

---

## Recommendations

- Introduce long-term supplier agreements to reduce purchase price volatility.
- Improve debtor collection strategies.
- Align receivable and payable cycles.
- Upgrade coal storage facilities to minimize moisture damage.
- Prioritize high-margin products.
- Monitor liquidity through regular financial ratio analysis.
